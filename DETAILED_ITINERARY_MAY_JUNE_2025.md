### Trip Overview
- **Travelers**: 2 people (one from Seattle, one 80-year-old active mom from Toronto)
- **Duration**: 27 days
- **Route**: Phoenix, AZ to Glacier National Park, MT (one-way)
- **Total Estimated Driving**: ~2,200 miles
- **Estimated Total Cost**: $8,500-$12,000 for two people

## ESSENTIAL PRE-TRIP RESERVATIONS CHECKLIST

### CRITICAL - Book 2-4 Months in Advance:
1. **Antelope Canyon Tours** - Book immediately (sells out months ahead)
   - Upper Antelope Canyon: www.antelopecanyon.com
   - Lower Antelope Canyon: www.antelopelowercanyon.com

2. **Arches National Park Timed Entry** (May 22)
   - Book at Recreation.gov starting January 2, 2025
   - $2 per reservation (non-refundable)
   - Required 7 AM - 4 PM, April 1 - July 6, 2025

3. **Glacier National Park Vehicle Reservation** (June 7-10)
   - Book at Recreation.gov starting February 13, 2025
   - $2 per reservation
   - Required June 13 - September 28, 2025, 7 AM - 3 PM for West Entrance
   - **NOTE**: East entrance (St. Mary) does NOT require reservations

4. **Accommodations** - Book 2-3 months ahead for:
   - Grand Canyon South Rim (in-park lodges fill fast)
   - Page, AZ (Memorial Day weekend nearby)
   - Jackson Hole (popular destination)
   - Glacier area (limited options)

### IMPORTANT PASSES TO PURCHASE:
**America the Beautiful Senior Pass** (for 80-year-old mom):
- **Lifetime Pass**: $80 (covers entrance to ALL national parks)
- **Annual Pass**: $20
- Purchase at any park entrance or online at store.usgs.gov
- **BONUS**: 50% discount on camping at federal campgrounds
- This pass will save you $200+ on this trip!

### Antelope Canyon Details

**WHICH CANYON TO VISIT?**

**Upper Antelope Canyon:**
- Pros: Famous light beams (best 10:30 AM - 1:30 PM, March-October), easier to walk
- Cons: More crowded, shorter time in canyon
- Accessibility: Sandy floor, relatively flat, some uneven spots
- Duration: ~1.5 hours total (40 min driving, ~60 min in canyon)
- **BEST FOR**: Iconic light beam photos, less physical ability required

**Lower Antelope Canyon:**
- Pros: Longer canyon, less crowded, beautiful formations
- Cons: Requires climbing ladders/stairs (may not be suitable for 80-year-old)
- Accessibility: Multiple ladder climbs required
- Duration: ~1.5 hours
- **NOT RECOMMENDED** for travelers with mobility concerns

**RECOMMENDATION**: Book **Upper Antelope Canyon** for an 80-year-old traveler

**TOUR OPERATORS (Upper Antelope Canyon):**

1. **Antelope Canyon Tours** (Official)
   - Website: www.antelopecanyon.com
   - Address: 22 S Lake Powell Blvd, Page, AZ 86040

2. **Antelope Canyon Navajo Tours**
   - Website: www.navajoantelopecanyon.com

### Horseshoe Bend

**Location**: US-89, 5 miles south of Page

**Trail Details:**
- Distance: 1.5 miles round trip
- Elevation Change: ~200 feet (downhill to viewpoint, uphill return)
- Surface: Paved walkway (recently improved!)
- Shade: None - bring hat, sunscreen, water
- Railings: Yes, at the viewpoint
- Time Needed: 45-60 minutes

**Best Time to Visit:**
- Morning (before 10 AM) or late afternoon (after 4 PM)
- Avoid midday - extremely hot, harsh light for photos
- Sunset is spectacular but very crowded

**Entry Fee**: $10/vehicle

**Accessibility Notes:**
- The trail is now paved and more accessible
- Still has some slope - 80-year-old can do this but take it slow
- Benches along the trail for rest
- Bring walking stick if helpful

### Monument Valley

**Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park**
- Address: Monument Valley, UT 84536
- Entry Fee: $20/vehicle (not covered by National Park passes)
- Hours: 6 AM - 8 PM (summer)
- Visitor Center has excellent viewpoint, gift shop, restrooms

**OPTIONS FOR VISITING:**

**Option 1: Viewpoint Only (Recommended for limited time)**
- Park at Visitor Center
- Walk to overlook platform
- Iconic view of mittens and buttes
- Time needed: 30-45 minutes
- Accessible

**Option 2: Self-Drive Scenic Loop (17 miles)**
- Unpaved road, rough in places
- Takes 2-3 hours with stops
- Check rental car policy (some prohibit)
- Not recommended for low-clearance vehicles
- Good for photography

**Option 3: Guided Tour (Best experience)**
- Navajo-led tours go to areas self-drivers cannot
- 1.5-hour tours: ~$65-85/person
- Book at Visitor Center or in advance
- Air-conditioned vehicles available
